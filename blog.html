Understanding Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs): A Mathematical Deep Dive

January 2025 | 15 min read | Neural Networks, Deep Learning, Mathematical Modeling

Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) represent a revolutionary approach to neural network architecture, drawing inspiration from the Kolmogorov-Arnold representation theorem. Unlike traditional Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) that use fixed activation functions on nodes, KANs place learnable activation functions on edges, fundamentally changing how neural networks learn and represent functions. This post provides a comprehensive mathematical deep dive into KANs, exploring their theoretical foundations, architecture details, training procedures, and advantages over traditional MLPs.
